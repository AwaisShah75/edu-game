## The Whole App — 3 Steps

| Step | What the Teacher Does | What Happens on Screen |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — Upload | Open the website. Upload a chapter PDF. Pick Easy, Medium, or Hard. | AI reads the PDF and generates questions in seconds. |
| 2 — Edit | Look through the questions. Delete bad ones. Add your own if needed. | A clean list of questions ready to use. |
| 3 — Play | Pick a game mode. Click Play. Display on the smartboard. | Game runs fullscreen on the smartboard. Class plays together. |

> There are no other steps. No login. No saving. No sending anything to students.

---

## How Children Play — On the Smartboard

Everything happens on one screen — the smartboard. Children do NOT need phones, tablets, or any device. The teacher controls the game from the laptop or by tapping the smartboard directly.

| Game Mode | How Children Participate |
|---|---|
| Quiz (MCQs) | Question and 4 options shown big on smartboard. Teacher calls a child's name. Child comes up and taps the answer — or calls it out and teacher clicks it. |
| True or False | Statement shown on smartboard. Teacher asks the class. Children raise hands or shout. Teacher taps True or False. |
| Tug of War — 2 Teams | Class split into Team A and Team B. Question appears. First team to call the correct answer pulls the rope. Animated rope moves on the smartboard. |
| Memory Game | Card grid shown on smartboard. Children take turns coming up to tap two cards to find a matching pair. |
| Spin the Wheel | Teacher taps Spin. Wheel spins and lands on a question. A child answers it. |
| Flashcards | Cards shown one by one. Teacher taps to flip. Class reads and answers together. |

## Screen by Screen

### Screen 1 — Home
One big upload box in the middle of the page. Nothing else.
- A button to upload a PDF
- Three buttons: **Easy — Medium — Hard**
- A **Generate Questions** button

### Screen 2 — Question Editor
A list of all AI-generated questions. Teacher reviews before playing.
- Each question shown with its correct answer and wrong options
- A **Delete** button next to each question
- An **Add Question** button at the bottom to type a custom question
- An **Edit** button to change any question's wording
- A **Choose Game** button when ready

### Screen 3 — Choose Game
Six large colourful buttons, one per game. Teacher taps one.
- Quiz — MCQs
- True or False
- Tug of War
- Memory Game
- Spin the Wheel
- Flashcards

### Screen 4 — Game (Fullscreen on Smartboard)
The game takes over the full screen. Big, bright, readable from the back of the classroom.
- All text large enough to read from 5 metres away
- Bright colours and simple layout for young children
- Teacher taps **Next** to move to the next question
- A **Restart** button available at any time
- A **Back to Home** button at the end of the game

## Difficulty Levels

| Level | What the AI Does |
|---|---|
| Easy | Very short simple questions. One answer is clearly correct. Good for Grade 1–2. |
| Medium | Normal grade-level questions. Some thinking needed. Good for Grade 3–4. |
| Hard | Tricky questions with close options. Requires strong understanding. Good for Grade 5. |

- No saved quizzes — each session starts fresh from a PDF
- No scores saved or tracked
- No reports or exports
- No dashboard
- No settings page
- No installation — just open a website
- No cost — now or ever
